Predicting Stock Trends with Machine Learning¶

Authors: Bruce Jiang, Joshua Elubiaozor

Introduction¶

From the big screen with movies such as The Wolf on Wall Street to dinner room table conversations, it seems that making it big using the stock market is its own American dream. Heavily fantasized, the reality is "...as much as 90% of people lose their money in stock markets..." (https://www.researchandranking.com/blog/shocking-but-true-90-people-lose-money-in-stocks). However, equipped with technology previously unavailable years ago, would it be possible to use machine learning and complex algorithms at least mitigate the risk, or even accurately predict stock market trends/movements?

For general knowledge, the Adjusted Price of a stock, amends a stock's closing price to reflect that stock's value after accounting for any corporate actions. The adjusted closing price factors in corporate actions, such as stock splits, dividends, and rights offerings. T learn more, you can check out https://www.thebalance.com/what-is-adjusted-closing-price-5190242. It might be helpful to understand that little piece of information because the algorithms is developed on Adjusted Closing prices.

Data¶

Gathering Processing¶

Yahoo! Finance provides a straightforward method of obtaining a csv that contains open, high, low, close, adjusted close, and volume for any given date from IPO.

How to obtain CSV¶

  1. Go to https://finance.yahoo.com/
  2. In the search bar, enter desired stock's symbol/ticker
  3. Select the historical data tab
  4. Click on download Note: Choose any time period you desire, for this tutorial we'll be working with IPO

Model¶

Now that we have our data ready, we can proceed to move on to creating a model that will predict stock movements. We'll be using an LTSM Network, or Long Short-Term Memory Network. We'll be using LTSM for a few reasons. LTSM Networks are capable of making predictions over time, as well as being relatively insensitive to gaps in time.

Further information on LTSM can be found here: https://colah.github.io/posts/2015-08-Understanding-LSTMs/

Before we can insert our data into our model, we should split our data into training and test data. We're going to use a test size of 15%, but for higher accuracy you can always decrease the parameter.

Our data seems to be in order, which means we can now move onto building our model.

As we start building our model, first thing we do is to add layers to our model. We add the LSTM layer with 32 neurons and because this will serve as our first layer, next we add an input shape. With our input shape, we add a number to time steps which is 1 and the number of features which is 1. We are not adding anymore LSTM models, therefore we do return_sequences=False. To help our models accuracy, we add a Dense layer with 1 neuron.

OBSERVATION

After analysis, we have noticed that although the LSTM model follows the general trends of the graph, it sometimes over/underestimates the prices greatly.

GOAL

Our goal was to analyze stock history with two different types of models. In this next part we will be using more layers of an LSTM model than our previous solution. This model will have an additional Dropout layer to further help to prevent overfitting. On our first model, we noticed that our model ws overfitting the data that we had even after training the model multiple times. Using a different and more extensive use of data. We will create and load a new stock by the ticker symbol RIOT for the company RIOT.
